What California DROP is

According to the California Privacy Protection Agency, DROP is the Delete Request and Opt-out Platform. It is a free California-run tool that lets eligible California residents send one request to more than 500 registered data brokers. The state says data brokers begin processing DROP requests on August 1, 2026.

Who it is for

California residents

Eligibility is based on California residency. The official site says some requests can also be submitted for another California resident in certain cases, such as a child or elderly relative.

What it covers

Registered data brokers

DROP is aimed at registered data brokers, not every company on the internet. It helps with deletion and no-sale requests in that specific part of the privacy ecosystem.

What to expect

Not instant, not universal

The state says brokers start processing on August 1, 2026, then delete data within 90 days, with later deletion cycles every 45 days. That is powerful, but it is not universal deletion from all companies.

FAQ

Can PrivacyGuard submit a DROP request for me?

No claim like that is being made in this version. PrivacyGuard is a guidance product. The public site points you to the official California tool and helps you understand the workflow.

Who can use California DROP?

The official California guidance says you must be a California resident to submit a DROP deletion request. The state also says some people may submit for another California resident in certain cases.

Does DROP delete my data everywhere?

No. The official materials describe DROP as a request system for registered data brokers. It is not a universal delete-everything button for every website, app, or data holder.

What are the privacy trade-offs?

You still provide personal information to complete matching and verification. The state says the platform is built to protect that information, but any deletion workflow involves sharing enough information to identify your records.

Is this legal advice?

No. PrivacyGuard provides educational guidance and practical workflow support, not legal advice or legal representation.
